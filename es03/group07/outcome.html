About

The Great Amazonian Barrage is the result of a long exploration of water related struggles as part of the Final Synthesis Studio (C3) of the Master Degree in Communication Design at Politecnico di Milano. After a thorough data-driven analysis of water conflicts, a Digital Methods driven analysis of dam controversies led to the creation of this website, aimed at raising awareness on the impacts of hydroelectric power through an exemplary case study: Belo Monte in Brazil.

Was your choice difficult?
What you have experienced has happened and is going to happen many times again, for every single dam that is planned to be built in Brazil. The Belo Monte case is soon to be closed: whatever its outcome, it will set a precedent for future environmental justice appeals. This concerns all of us, and our planet.

Wild Card:
The Future

The new President Jair Bolsonaro, on the day of his official investiture, stripped the agency in charge of indigenous affairs of the power to demarcate indigenous lands with his first decree. Instead, the agriculture ministry will take over those functions. Today, Bolsonaro's Brazil seems to be a fertile land for businesses and enterprise who wish to maximise the exploitation of Amazonian natural resources, stomping over environmental rights.
