WHAT WAS THE LAST TIME YOU SAW A MALAYSIAN CHINESE WORKING IN CIVIL SERVICE?

Despite making up almost a quarter of the population, in 2010 only 5.8% of Malay’s civil service was ethnically Chinese.

2016, at present, civil servants in Malaysia account for 95% of indigenous people, and Chinese and Indians only account for 5%.

“Chinese do not like to be civil servants?”

In 2010, the Malaysian authorities recruited 40 Chinese-investigators to the Chinese community and had attracted 405 applicants. Their response was so enthusiastic that it was enough to dispute the surmise.

Data source: Wangyi News, The McGill International Review

IS IT MORE EXPENSIVE FOR THE NON-INDIGENOUS TO BUY A HOUSE?

Since 1971, real state developers have had to retain at least 30% of their units in the residential or commercial sector to increase the ownership of indigenous.

In addition to the NewEconomic Policy (NEP), the purchases of indigenous units are subject to a 10% to 15% discount on the original price.

“The State Housing and Real Estate Commissioner, after research, believes that some constituencies are mostly Chinese, and have made the decision to abolish the establishment of indigenous houses. At the same time, the committee will also be in some indigenous constituencies such as Gaowu. , Wanneng, Yili, etc., to increase the number of indigenous shophouses to 40% to achieve a balance."

Data source: ChinaQw, Kanproperty, Sinchew

IS THE SITUATION OF CHINESE AND TAMIL PRIMARY SCHOOLS WORRYING?

Primary school students accounted for 76.4% of the total number in the country, but the allocation of education bureaus accounted for 96.06% of them. In contrast, Chinese primary school students accounted for 20.93%, but the allocation was only 2.73%.

Tamil primary schools is one of the school types that the students select basically according to their ethnicity in Malaysia which are mostly made up of Indian Children. However, from 1957 to 2012, the number of these schools has been declined from 888 to 523.

Data source: ShangGuanXiaoHong

DO RELIGIOUS SENSITIVES STILL EXIST?
